It goes far beyond basic technical proficiency; it requires the critical thinking to identify credible sources in an era of AI-generated content and the ethical awareness to behave responsibly in online spaces.

Part A: 
Cybercrime Case Study

We took a look at a specific online threat and wrote about it in the following directory:

task-5-cybercrime/casestudy.md

We focused on UPI and Online Payment Fraud.

This is when someone takes money from your bank account without permission using the UPI system.

This usually happens when someone tricks you into thinking you are getting money. Really they are taking it. They might send you a message saying you won a prize or they might use a bad QR code.

The people who are most likely to be targeted are students and young professionals in India who use wallets and payment apps.

If you are a victim of this crime you could lose money away and the bad people might also get into your bank account. This can be very stressful

Part B:
Stay Safe Online Checklist

We made a list of ways for college students to stay safe online. You can find it in:

task-5-cybercrime/prevention-checklist.md

Here are some important things to do to stay safe:

Enable Two-Factor Authentication on your email and social media accounts.

Use a hard-to-guess password for each account and change them every few months.

When using UPI, never enter your PIN to get money. You only need your PIN to send money or check your balance.

When using UPI make sure you know who you are sending money to before you authorize the transaction.

Do not click on links that are shortened or that people send you in messages that you did not ask for.

Do not use Wi-Fi to do your banking or to get into sensitive school websites.

Check which apps have access to your camera, contacts or location and take away that access if they do not need it.

Keep your device and antivirus software up to date so you can fix any security problems.
